Lasagna Recipe: A Culinary Classic

Description

Lasagna, a quintessential Italian dish, has transcended its regional origins to become a beloved comfort food worldwide. Its layers of pasta, rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and molten cheese create a harmonious blend of flavors and textures that delight the palate. This recipe aims to guide you through the process of creating a traditional lasagna, emphasizing the importance of quality ingredients and careful preparation.

Ingredients

  • For the Meat Sauce:

    • 1 lb ground beef
    • 1 lb Italian sausage, casing removed
    • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 large carrot, finely chopped
    • 2 cans (28 oz each) crushed tomatoes
    • 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste
    • 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce
    • 1/2 cup red wine
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 tablespoon sugar
    • 2 teaspoons dried basil
    • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
    • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  • For the Béchamel Sauce:

    • 4 cups whole milk
    • 6 tablespoons butter
    • 6 tablespoons all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 teaspoon grated nutmeg
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  • For the Assembly:

    • 1 package lasagna noodles
    • 16 oz ricotta cheese
    • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
    • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Steps

  1. Prepare the Meat Sauce

    1. Brown the Meat: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground beef and Italian sausage, breaking them up with a wooden spoon. Cook until browned and no longer pink. Remove the meat from the pot and set aside, draining any excess fat.
    2. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pot, add the chopped onion, carrot, and garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are tender and the onion is translucent, about 5-7 minutes.
